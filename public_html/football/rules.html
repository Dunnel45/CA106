Rules and Regulations

  • Game

    A football match is 90 minutes long, it is split up into 2 45 minute halfs.
    Any stoppage time is added on by the referee at the end of the 90 minutes.
    The aim of the game is to score more goals than your opponent in the 90 minutes.

  • Players and equipment

    There are 11 players on a team, which consist of 1 goalkeeper and 10 outfield players.
    The pitch is split up into 2 identical halfs in which there is:

    1. A 6 yard box next to the opening of the goal
    2. An 18 yard box around the 6 yard box
    3. A centre circle

    Players wear studded football boots, shin guards, matching jerseys, shorts and socks.
    The goalkeeper wears gloves as they can touch the ball with their hands.

  • Goals

    To score the ball must go over the line of the opposition's goal. All of the ball needs to be past the line for the goal to count. A goal can be scored with any body part except the hand

  • Offside rule

    An attacking player is deemed offside when the player is in front of the last defender when the ball is passed to them by a team member.
    A player cannot be offside in their own half or when the ball is passed to them by a player on the other team.
    This rule was enforced to stop attacking players from staying behind the other team's defence and scoring unfair goals.
