Youth Programme

The GAA’s vision is ‘that everybody has the opportunity to be welcomed to take part in our games and culture, to participate fully, to grow and develop and to be inspired to keep a lifelong engagement with our association.’ (The GAA Strategic Action Plan 2015-2017) However recent research and GAA registration figures are indicating that there is a steady decline in the number of GAA youth level players from the age of 12 on.

The GAA & GPA All Stars Young Footballer of the Year (often called the All Stars Young Footballer of the Year, or simply Young Footballer of the Year) is an annual award given at the end of the Championship season to a young footballer who is adjudged to have been the best in Gaelic football.

Fun Fact:

Youth players get the opportunity to play for their county as early as U14 where they will be in competiton with the best gaa players their age around the country!
