Current Day

2017 was a good year for Irish horse racing with nineteen Irish-trained winners at the Cheltenham festival setting the tone for a year of international success.

The Melbourne Cup was won by trainer Joseph O’Brien, and a world record 28 Group and Grade 1 Flat winners for his father Aidan in 2017.

Irish-bred horses continued to dominate at the highest levels internationally and the value of Irish horse exports sold at public auction was €271.5m.


Attendance

Racecourse attendances in 2017 decreased by 3%, impacted by the reduced numbers at the Curragh, in particular the Dubai Duty Free Irish Derby and day two of the Longines Irish Champions Weekend but this was mostly due to weather over these two days.

Attendances at the major jumps festivals in the first six months of 2017 were very strong. Punchestown’s Spring Festival attracted a record attendance of 122,557.
